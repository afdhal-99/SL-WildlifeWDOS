Efforts to combat poaching :
Illegal wildlife trade threatening leopards involve a combination of legislative measures, law enforcement, and community engagement. Countries, including Sri Lanka, implement strict wildlife protection laws and penalties for trafficking leopard parts. Increased patrolling in critical habitats helps deter poachers, while intelligence-led operations target illegal wildlife trade networks. Public awareness campaigns educate communities about the importance of leopard conservation, fostering local support. Collaboration with international organizations and advocacy for strengthened regulations contribute to a comprehensive strategy aimed at reducing the threat of poaching and illegal wildlife trade impacting leopard populations.
